AI Is Ruining Your Mind

It feels kinda weird for me to say this, but I am a professional in the field now. Woooo. Just ignore the part of my title that says "Intern"—Just ignore it. K thanks. Anyway, after working for almost 4 months now, I can confidently tell you that AI not there yet. I'm not going to pretend I never use AI, sometimes I need to look at a reference if I can't find one. Or maybe I need to get some foundational work out of the way quickly and I know exactly how I need it structured. Nine times out of ten, I would just do it myself because I don't trust AI enough to do it correctly. This isn't me knocking anyone for using AI, everyone has the choice to use or avoid it. This is more about how I believe AI is still incompetent and it is nowhere near as intelligent as these AI shills would let you let you believe. I'm not sitting here using every day, so some of my claims might be wrong and I am very open to criticism and corrections. But something I strongly believe in is that if you're scared that AI is going to replace you, you have already been replaced. Also shout-out to that accounting student that looked me dead in the eyes and told me my field was going to be replaced but his field couldn't be replace by AI.

It's All About Money

I'm going to try and keep this portion semi-brief because I believe the title is sufficient to my point. Most people you see on LinkedIn talking about how smart AI is and how you can do the work of many or become an engineer in a matter of days are exaggerating. AI is still an remarkable feat that we made, but it doesn't change the fact it was never good enough to do the work of a senior engineer, let a lone a JUNIOR engineer. They mostly have some stake in an AI company or ChatGPT wrapper. I'm not saying the last point as an insult, people are still making ChatGPT wrappers. Many of these people just want someone to invest or subscribe to their service. The same can be said for any platform you go on. There are people who are just wowed by AI and everything it can do, it's very impressive how fast it progressed. I just wish data centers weren't consuming all the water and power. This is not a joke, the electric bill went up in my area. When you are browsing the any social media platform, just be diligent. A lot of these people promoting AI are equivalent to snake oil salesmen. I will not name this person for very obvious reasons, but someone I just so happen to be following started an AI company. What's their product? I couldn't tell you and that's not important. What's important is their credentials—The ones they do not have. They worked at three different internships for three months and they changed the title of their positions to something that I'm pretty sure are not real. It's either that or they were fired which is much worse. So be careful when you see someone promoting AI.

AI Cannot And Will Not Replace You

This is something strange that I see people believing. AI is going to replace me? Me? The data engineer? That's a load of baloney. I have enough faith in myself to debug or solve a problem without AI. If you feel like this is hard—that being coding or any sort of problem solving without AI—then this is the time to learn. People are still struggling to find jobs not because AI is replacing people, but they just rely on what school teaches them. You haven't been replace by AI, you're in a very competitive field. I know there are more reasons behind the difficulty to the job search, but I'm not going to blame something as inept as AI for denying me any job. Though, it might not replace you, but it could block you. Ignoring this completely dystopian fact, AI is as smart as the user. It does not take months to become an engineer, it takes years. Just because I can ask an AI chatbot for legal advice doesn't make me a lawyer. Just because AI can spit out the same garbage website with that really nice gradient of purple doesn't make you a web-dev. You cannot suddenly take my role with zero knowledge of the field. This goes for anything—except for accounting. I don't actually believe that, but if anything is going to be replace first, it would be accountants. Kidding aside, you are going to need to make yourself stand out. There are so many free resources, you just need to look. If you're not interested in reading up in tech you want to learn, as the kids say, tuff. Don't shoot yourself in the foot, start learning on your own and make projects. This is how I ended up as a data engineer. I am going to reiterate, if you feel like you're going to be replace by AI, you have already been replace. Either get good or stay in debt. Or if you're self taught, good luck.
