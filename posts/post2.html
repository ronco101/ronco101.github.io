Installing Linux

There is not a single thing on this planet that I can say to make this part exciting. Installing any new operating system is the equivalent to pulling out your own nails and teeth. Somehow, Windows has the WORST installer I have ever used. This isn't me saying Linux has the good installer, the windows installer requires you to have a Microsoft account. There are ways around it, but I should not have to open up the terminal using Shift+F10 and type in "oobe\bypassnro" to bypass the login. Regardless, the install process is so mindnumbing, I'm just used to whatever crap I get from it now. Did you know your system can fail to install? I DO NOW. After running the installer again because I wiped my laptop hard drive, it worked. Yippee. I installed EndeavourOS, it's Arch with a coat of paint on it. It's a very good "beginner" system, it's for people who are looking something more complicated than Ubuntu/Debian without going through the Arch install process. You can tell me I need to install base or vanilla arch, I do not care. I just want a computer I can do my work on. That was until I destroyed my system because I can never have anything nice in my life. I then moved on to mint. Somehow, an even worse install experience than the EndeavourOS. Clearly didn't work out for me, so I moved to Manjaro. Wow, that's cool. Anyway, don't use Manjaro. And here I am today using CachyOS. Do I even like it??? Yeah, it just works and I'm not running into any serious issues yet, despite being on Wayland. If you don't know what Wayland is, I'm begging you to just stop asking questions. It does not matter, if you use your computer to watch YouTube or do some light gaming, you do not need to know what Wayland or X11 is for your own sanity. After becoming a masochist, no sane person does what I did, I can tell you with great confidence that Windows still has the worst install experience I have ever gone through.

Everything just works?

If you know nothing about Linux, this might not make that much sense to you. I'm talking 10 years ago now, Linux was not plug and play. It is insane to me that I was able to plug in my drawing tablet and have it work automatically. Of course I needed to install some drivers to use the buttons, but it worked perfectly. KDE Plasma, while some people hate it for good and weird reasons, might be one of the best desktop experiences you can ever get on a computer. Obviously, you can construct or find better configurations for a desktop environment, but I'm not talking about that. I'm not talking about the team behind it either, I'm talking about the fact that everything worked as intended with little or zero input. You really start to wonder who the fuck was using Linux before hand? The answer is people smarter than you or I. Unless you are a Kernel developer or Linus Torval himself. They are the ones maintaining and made Linux respectively, if they don't know what they're doing, god help us all. I feel like I have to mention that I'm kidding now despite my original warning. Linux was usable, but never to the extent of just plugging something in and having it work immediately. Also want to give a shoutout to possibly the only person on this planet to use uEmacs, Linus Torval. He is the only one maintaining it and says that he's too old for syntax highlighting. I'm not even making fun of him, imagine someone told you that, "Yeah, I maintain an abandoned text editor that I use to write code for the Linux Kernel." What the fuck do you say to that? Nothing you can say or do will ever be as cool as that, no personal project can compete. Well, it's better at least much better than Ed, the text editor. Not Ed, from Ed, Edd, And Eddie.
